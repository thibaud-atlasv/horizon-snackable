# Action-Adventure Greyboxing

Priorities for action-adventure level blockouts (God of War, Zelda style).

## Pacing Curve
Alternate beat types in sequence:
- Combat arena → traversal → puzzle → narrative moment → rest
- Use color-coded boxes during blockout: red=combat, blue=puzzle, green=story

## Combat Arenas
- Ensure dodge/roll distances work within space
- Provide flanking routes for enemies
- Include environmental hazards or interactables
- Test camera behavior in confined spaces

## Traversal & Puzzles
- Prove puzzle is solvable AND readable before visual noise
- Test climbing/jumping with exact player metrics
- Ensure backtracking routes don't feel tedious

## Camera Framing
- Block "vista moments" where camera pulls back for reveals
- Test narrative beats with placeholder camera positions
- Ensure combat cameras don't clip geometry

## Validation Method
Walk through full sequence noting:
- Time between combat encounters
- Puzzle solve time (first attempt vs. repeat)
- Emotional pacing: tension → release rhythm

## Checklist
- [ ] Pacing beats alternate correctly
- [ ] Combat spaces support full moveset
- [ ] Puzzles solvable without visual hints
- [ ] Vista/reveal moments blocked
- [ ] Backtracking feels purposeful
