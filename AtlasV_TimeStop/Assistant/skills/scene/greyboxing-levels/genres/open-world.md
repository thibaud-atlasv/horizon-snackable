# Open World Greyboxing

Priorities for open world level blockouts (Elden Ring, Breath of the Wild style).

## Landmark Visibility
- Can players see points of interest from multiple angles?
- Do landmarks read at distance?
- Is the horizon compelling?

## Traversal Investment
- Is the journey to a location rewarding, not tedious?
- Are there micro-discoveries along paths?
- Does elevation change create varied experience?

## Density Tuning
- How far apart should encounters/discoveries be?
- Test "nothing happening" zones—are they restful or boring?
- Balance density vs. breathing room

## Vertical Navigation
- Test climbing/gliding/mounting across elevation changes
- Ensure vertical shortcuts feel earned
- Validate fall damage/safety zones

## Slice Methodology
Build one region fully before expanding. Open worlds are expensive to iterate—prove the formula in a vertical slice first.

## Validation Method
Time player journeys between landmarks. Track:
- Discoveries per minute of travel
- "I want to go there" moments
- Navigation confusion points

## Checklist
- [ ] Landmarks visible from multiple approaches
- [ ] Traversal has micro-rewards
- [ ] Density tested for pacing
- [ ] Vertical movement validated
- [ ] One slice proven before expansion
