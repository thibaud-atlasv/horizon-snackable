# Horror Greyboxing

Priorities for horror/survival horror level blockouts.

## Tension Through Restriction
- Narrow corridors, limited sightlines
- One-way doors and irreversible progression points
- Dead ends that force confrontation

## Spatial Contrast
- Safe rooms feel different: higher ceilings, more open
- Transition spaces signal mood shifts
- Compression before expansion creates relief

## Audio Planning
- Plan where sounds bleed through vs. muffle
- Block audio occlusion geometry early
- Test footstep echo in different space sizes

## Resource Scarcity
- Place pickups and verify backtracking distances
- Ensure resource placement creates difficult choices
- Test with minimum viable resources

## Lighting Consideration
- Playtest in low lighting even at greybox stage
- Darkness fundamentally changes spatial perception
- Block light sources and shadow zones

## Validation Method
Playtest in dim lighting with placeholder audio. Note:
- Heart rate moments (subjective tension peaks)
- Relief moments (safe room discovery)
- Resource anxiety points

## Checklist
- [ ] Sightlines restricted appropriately
- [ ] Safe rooms contrast with threat spaces
- [ ] Audio occlusion planned
- [ ] Resource placement tested
- [ ] Lighting zones blocked
