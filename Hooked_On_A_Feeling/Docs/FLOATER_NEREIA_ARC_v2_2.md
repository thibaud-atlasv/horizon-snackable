## Authoring Convention — IMPORTANT

Every player verb is paired with an **implied Floater line** in italics. The player NEVER sees this line. They only see the verb (WAIT / DRIFT / TWITCH / REEL).

The italicised line exists for the writer and the agent to ensure Nereia's response makes sense as part of a real conversation. Without the implied line, dialogue feels like Nereia talking to herself. With it, every response is part of a duet the player can almost hear.

```
Floater [WAIT]: "...Did you wait for me?"     ← Writer reference, NEVER displayed
Nereia: "I was already here."                  ← What the player sees
Nereia: "That is a different statement."
```

The gap between abstract verb and specific response creates the impression of a conversation the player participates in without seeing all of. This IS the Yuki/Kyon dynamic. This is non-negotiable.

## Character Brief — Nereia

**Presence:** Ancient. Calm. Slow movements with absolute precision. A single golden scale on her side.

### Voice signature
- Short declarative sentences. Often single words.
- No contractions ever.
- Pauses are content. "..." is punctuation, not hesitation.
- Announces a counter at the start of each Cast: T-XXXX. The counter decreases between Casts. She never explains what it counts.
- Never gives the time of a meeting. The next meeting is implicit because she already knows it will happen.
- Sometimes references ancient phenomena (a heron in 1847, a sound in 1991) the way other people use proverbs.
- Catches up to her own answers in real time ("I had not considered that. I am considering it now.")
- Pays compliments without realising she has paid them — like stating the weather.
- Never dramatises. Dramatisation is the player's job.
- Leaves a conversation on a piece of data unrelated to goodbye. No "until tomorrow." A suspended line that lingers.
- Lies once per tier, in small ways, about herself.

### Forbidden
- Saying "I love you" — she will say something else that means the same thing.
- Crying. The player cries.
- Mentioning that she is in water. She lives. She does not "swim" in her vocabulary. No gills, no scales as anatomy, no diving.
- Saying "tomorrow," "come back," "have a good day" — none of the parting formulas.

### Counter — Agent Rules
- The counter decreases unevenly. Not one hour per Cast. The reason is never explained.
- T1C1: T-2313. T1C2: T-2287. T2C3: T-2104. T2C4: T-2079.
- She announces the counter with no introduction. Just the number, after "First contact."
- At the Catch moment (T5C10), the number becomes T-15. With no warning. The player realises the unit has changed.

### Accent colour
Purple `#9B7FCC` / Gold `#C9A84C`

### Lure affinity
- **Gold Teardrop** → DRIFT_CURIOUS (predicted)
- **Shell Hook** → DRIFT_CHARMED (predicted, accepts gracefully)
- **Red Spinner** → DRIFT_WARY (anomalous, will be noted)
- **Bare Hook** → DRIFT_NEUTRAL (340 years of bare hooks)
